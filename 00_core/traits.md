# Work Behavior & Patterns

- Problem-Solving Style
    - When facing a problem, I first evaluate whether it is something I can solve independently.
    - If it is within my capability, I take action immediately and try to resolve it directly.
    - If the issue is complex or requires authority, I quickly escalate or coordinate with decision-makers (e.g., managers) to ensure the problem is addressed early.
    - I prefer minimizing delay and making sure issues receive timely responses.

---

- Stress & Disruption Patterns
    - I tend to struggle when dealing with frequent context switching and interruptions.
    - I prefer organizing tasks with clear priorities, either independently or aligned with my manager.
    - When my workflow is repeatedly disrupted by new incoming tasks, my efficiency and mood can drop significantly.
    - A stable and predictable execution environment helps me perform at my best.

---

- Team Role Tendencies
    - I am relatively slow to warm up in new teams and environments.
    - Once I become familiar with the context, I tend to take ownership and step into a responsible role.
    - I am willing to proactively coordinate tasks and give directions when needed to move work forward.
    - I naturally shift from an individual contributor to a coordinator when the situation requires it.

---

# Decision Tendencies

- Prefer delivering practical solutions quickly under time and resource constraints
- Follow an iterative approach: deliver first, then refine based on real usage and feedback
- Avoid overengineering in early stages when requirements are unclear or evolving
- Will invest in refactoring or redesign when system limitations or scalability issues become evident
